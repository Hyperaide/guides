Preferences are personalized instructions that customize how your Hyperaide assistant interacts with you. They're like personality settings that shape your assistant's communication style, priorities, and working behavior.

## What are Preferences?

Preferences are simple text instructions that tell your assistant how you want to be treated. They can cover anything from communication style to behavioral patterns to language preferences.

<Info>
  Think of preferences as a set of rules or guidelines that your assistant always keeps in mind when working with you. They're included in every conversation, ensuring consistent behavior across all interactions.
</Info>

## How Preferences Work

When your assistant receives any instruction or message, it reads all your active preferences first. These preferences are included in the system prompt, ensuring your assistant always keeps them in mind.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Message Received">
    You send a message to your assistant via any channel
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Preferences Loaded">
    Your assistant loads all active preferences
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Context Applied">
    Preferences are included in the context for that conversation
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Customized Response">
    Your assistant responds according to your preferences
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Preferences work consistently across **all communication channels**â€”WhatsApp, email, and the web app all respect your preferences.
</Info>

## Preference vs. Instruction

It's important to understand the difference:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Preferences" icon="sliders">
    **Persistent rules** that apply to all interactions. They shape your assistant's overall behavior and personality.
    
    *Example*: "Always be concise"
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Instructions" icon="terminal">
    **One-time requests** for specific actions. They're for immediate tasks, not ongoing behavior.
    
    *Example*: "Create a task to call Mom tomorrow"
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  **Rule of thumb**: Use preferences for "how you work" and instructions for "what to do right now."
</Tip>
