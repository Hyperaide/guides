## Setting Up Google Calendar

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Integrations">
    Go to **Settings → Integrations** in Hyperaide
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Google Calendar">
    Click the **Google Calendar** integration card
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Sign in with Google">
    Click **Connect** and sign in to your Google account
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Grant Permissions">
    Authorize Hyperaide to read and manage your calendar events
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Calendars">
    Select which calendars to sync (Work, Personal, etc.)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Start Scheduling">
    Your assistant can now manage your Google Calendar!
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can connect multiple Google accounts if you manage separate personal and work calendars.
</Tip>

## Smart Scheduling Features

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Natural Date/Time Parsing" icon="language">
    Your assistant understands flexible date expressions:
    
    - "tomorrow at 2pm"

    - "first Monday of next month"
    - "in 3 hours"
    - "end of this week"
    - "next Tuesday morning"
    
    No need for specific date formats!
  </Accordion>

    Your assistant infers appropriate durations:
    
    ```text
    "Schedule a quick sync" → 15-30 minutes
    "Block time for deep work" → 2-3 hours
    "Add a standup" → 15 minutes
    "Schedule a client meeting" → 1 hour
    ```

    Or specify explicitly: "2-hour meeting"
  </Accordion>

    Your assistant warns about scheduling conflicts:
    
    ```text

    Assistant: "⚠️ You already have 'Budget Review' at 2pm. 
                Would you like to find another time?"
    ```
    
    Prevents double-booking automatically.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Time Zone Handling" icon="globe">
    Your assistant manages time zones seamlessly:
    
    - Displays times in your local timezone
    - Handles events with participants in other zones
    - Converts "9am PT" to your timezone
    - Respects Google Calendar timezone settings
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Permissions & Privacy

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What Hyperaide Can Access" icon="eye">
    - Read all events from selected calendars
    - View event details (title, time, attendees, location)
    - See free/busy status
    - Access calendar metadata
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What Hyperaide Can Do" icon="pen">
    - Create new events
    - Modify existing events
    - Delete events (when you request)
    - Accept/decline invitations
    
    Only when you explicitly instruct it.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="What Hyperaide Cannot Do" icon="shield-xmark">
    - Access calendars you didn't select
    - See Google account settings
    - Modify calendar sharing permissions
    - Access other Google services
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
  Google Calendar integration requires broad permissions to manage events. Only connect if you trust Hyperaide with your schedule.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Events Not Syncing" icon="arrows-rotate">
    **Solutions:**
    1. Check integration status in Settings
    2. Wait 30-60 seconds for sync
    3. Verify the calendar is selected for sync
    4. Try disconnecting and reconnecting
    5. Check Google Calendar itself is working
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can't Create Events" icon="calendar-xmark">
    **Solutions:**
    1. Verify you granted write permissions
    2. Check you're not trying to edit a shared calendar
    3. Ensure the calendar isn't read-only
    4. Reconnect with full permissions
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Wrong Time Zone" icon="clock">
    **Solutions:**
    1. Check your Google Calendar timezone settings
    2. Verify Hyperaide account timezone in Settings
    3. Be explicit: "Schedule at 2pm PT"
    4. Your assistant uses your account timezone by default
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Authorization Expired" icon="key">
    **Solution:**
    Google tokens expire for security:
    1. Go to Settings → Integrations
    2. Click **Reconnect** next to Google Calendar
    3. Re-authorize access
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
